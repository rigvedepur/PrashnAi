1. Water has a high specific heat, meaning it:
A) Heats and cools very quickly
B) Requires little energy for temperature change
C) Changes temperature slowly
D) Cannot store energy
Answer: C

2. The specific heat of water is high because of:
A) Weak hydrogen bonds
B) Strong hydrogen bonds
C) Ionic bonding
D) Heavy isotopes
Answer: B

3. Latent heat is defined as:
A) Heat felt directly as temperature
B) Heat transferred only by conduction
C) Heat released or absorbed during phase change
D) Solar energy at the surface
Answer: C

4. Sensible heat is:
A) Heat stored in water vapor
B) Heat you can feel and measure with a thermometer
C) Heat used for melting
D) Heat used for evaporation
Answer: B

5. When water evaporates, it:
A) Releases latent heat
B) Absorbs latent heat
C) Releases sensible heat
D) Gains density
Answer: B

6. When water vapor condenses into liquid, it:
A) Absorbs latent heat
B) Releases latent heat
C) Becomes less dense
D) Heats up the surrounding air by conduction only
Answer: B

7. The latent heat of vaporization is:
A) Heat needed to melt ice
B) Heat needed to freeze water
C) Heat needed to evaporate water
D) Heat needed to warm ice
Answer: C

8. The latent heat of fusion is:
A) Heat required to melt ice
B) Heat absorbed when vapor condenses
C) Heat used to evaporate water
D) Heat released during melting
Answer: A

9. Which phase change releases the MOST energy?
A) Melting
B) Freezing
C) Condensation
D) Evaporation
Answer: C

10. Evaporation causes cooling because:
A) Water gains density
B) Molecules with highest energy escape
C) Latent heat is released
D) Surface absorbs infrared radiation
Answer: B

11. Which process warms the surrounding environment?
A) Evaporation
B) Melting
C) Sublimation
D) Condensation
Answer: D

12. Density of water is highest at:
A) 0°C
B) 2°C
C) 3.98°C
D) 10°C
Answer: C

13. Ice floats on water because:
A) Ice is warmer
B) Ice is denser
C) Water expands when freezing
D) Ice contains trapped gases
Answer: C

14. Water’s high specific heat helps moderate:
A) Magnetic fields
B) Ocean salinity
C) Coastal temperatures
D) Air pressure changes
Answer: C

15. As water cools from 4°C to 0°C, its density:
A) Increases steadily
B) Decreases
C) Stays constant
D) Drops then rises

16. During sublimation, water changes from:
A) Ice to liquid
B) Vapor to liquid
C) Ice to vapor
D) Liquid to ice
Answer: C

17. Latent heat is stored mainly in:
A) Cold ocean currents
B) Water vapor
C) Sea ice
D) Cloud droplets
Answer: B

18. During deposition, water:
A) Absorbs heat
B) Releases heat
C) Transfers only sensible heat
D) Changes into liquid
Answer: B

19. Which process involves both latent heat release AND cloud formation?
A) Melting
B) Evaporation

D) Sublimation
Answer: C

20. Water vapor is important because it:
A) Is the least dense gas
B) Stores and transports latent heat

21. Which phase contains the MOST stored energy per gram?
A) Ice
B) Supercooled droplets

B) Temperature remains constant

A) Absorbs latent heat
